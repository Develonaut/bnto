# Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how bnto ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use bnto.io (the "Service").

---

## The Short Version

- **Client-side processing.** Many of our tools run entirely in your browser using WebAssembly. When a tool runs client-side, your files are processed on your device and are not uploaded to our servers.
- **Minimal data collection.** We collect only what's needed to provide and improve the service — an email address if you create an account, and usage data (which tools you use, when, and how long they take).
- **Analytics.** We use analytics to understand how the service is used, identify issues, and improve the experience for all users — including those on the free tier.
- **Open source engine.** Our core engine is MIT licensed and [publicly available](https://github.com/Develonaut/bnto).

---

## What We Collect

### Information You Provide

If you create an account, we collect your **email address** and **name**. You can use bnto without creating an account — all browser tools work without signing in.

### Information Collected Automatically

- **Anonymous sessions.** When you use bnto, we create an anonymous session to track your usage within that visit. This does not require any personal information.
- **Usage data.** We record which tools you use, when you use them, and how long processing takes. This helps us understand which tools are popular and identify performance issues.
- **Analytics data.** We use analytics tools to understand how people use the service, measure performance, and identify problems. This may include page views, feature usage, browser type, and general location (country/region level).
- **Standard web server logs.** Our hosting provider (Vercel) may collect standard web server information such as IP addresses, browser type, and pages visited. This is governed by [Vercel's Privacy Policy](https://vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy).

### Files and Client-Side Processing

Many bnto tools run entirely in your browser using WebAssembly. When a tool runs client-side, your files are processed on your device — they are not uploaded to or stored on our servers. Some tools or features may require server-side processing, in which case files are transmitted securely and are not retained after processing is complete.

## How We Use Your Information

- **Provide the service** — manage your account, track usage, and deliver features you've requested.
- **Improve the service** — understand which tools are most used, identify bugs, and improve performance.
- **Communicate with you** — send service notifications (such as changes to our terms or policies), product updates, and occasional announcements. You can unsubscribe from non-essential communications at any time.

---

## Cookies

We use cookies to provide and improve the service:

- **Authentication cookies.** Session cookies that keep you signed in. These are HttpOnly and secure — they cannot be read by JavaScript running on the page.
- **Sign-out signal cookie.** A short-lived cookie (expires in 10 seconds) used during the sign-out process to ensure a smooth redirect. It is automatically deleted.
- **Analytics cookies.** Our analytics tools may set cookies to understand how you use the service and to measure performance.
- **Theme preference.** Your light/dark mode preference is stored in your browser's local storage, not a cookie.

## Data Retention

- **Account data** is retained as long as your account is active. You can request deletion at any time.
- **Usage data** is retained for analytics and billing purposes. Anonymous usage data may be retained indefinitely in aggregate form.
- **Execution records** (which tool, when, duration) are retained as long as your account is active.

---

## Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information:

- Authentication tokens are stored in HttpOnly, secure cookies
- All traffic is encrypted via HTTPS
- Our backend (Convex) provides built-in access controls and data isolation
- Files processed by client-side tools stay on your device — the most secure data is data we never have

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.
